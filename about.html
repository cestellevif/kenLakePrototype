About Lakemoor Community Club

Our history, structure, and the people who make Ken Lake home.

Our Community

Ken Lake, West Olympia

Tucked away in the heart of West Olympia, the Lakemoor Community at Ken Lake is an urban forest community organized around a private natural lake.

In addition to protecting the lake and providing access through neighborhood parks, we come together several times each year for various events, such as the annual Fishing Derby and the 4th of July parade and picnic.

The Lakemoor Community at Ken Lake is a diverse, secure neighborhood which cherishes engagement and connection.

Governance

The LCC Board

Purpose

The LCC board manages our community resources to care for our lake, our shared public parks, and to fund community-led events.

Structure

Our HOA is managed by a volunteer board of directors who do the essential work of organizing our community. There are 7 members on the board, with 3 or 4 members elected into office each year. Learn more about board structure by reviewing our bylaws.

The work of the board may be carried out by board members, or by committees. Committees are formed by neighbors around goals or tasks, and are approved by the board. You can join a committee to share your experience, or to volunteer in small ways — we all work together to care for Ken Lake.

Community Safety

Ken Lake Security

“Ken Lake Security has served the residents of Ken Lake since May 2005. It is my goal to keep Ken Lake a great place to live and play. It is my duty to see that the residents of Ken Lake are as secure and safe as possible.”
— Ralph Oliver, Ken Lake Security

As part of the special focus on enforcing community rules, compliance checks are conducted in recreation areas during the high use periods. Ken Lake Security also monitors vehicle speeds year-round to help keep our residents safe.

A close association has been developed between Ken Lake Security and the Olympia Police Department to provide a high level of security at all times.

Personal Safety Guidelines

🚗

Neighborhood Safety

Speed limit is 25 mph. Follow parking, biking, walking, and pet rules to keep everyone safe on neighborhood streets.

🚤

Lake Safety

Follow water safety guidelines. Supervise swimmers at all times. Life jackets required on the water. Observe designated fishing zones.

🥾

Trail Safety

Hike and jog with a partner when possible. Stay on marked trails in the Urban Forest. Let someone know your route.

🏠

Home Safety

Keep fire extinguishers accessible and current. Have an escape plan and consider an escape ladder for upper floors.

🚨

Emergency

In an emergency, call 911. When calling for help, ask for a specific individual to assist. Consider personal safety devices and panic buttons.

Environmental Stewardship

Flora, Fauna & Yard Care

As an urban forest community, we are stewards of the natural environment that surrounds Ken Lake. Responsible landscaping, native plantings, and wildlife-friendly yard care help protect the lake and ecosystem.

Native Plants

Planting native species supports local wildlife and reduces water usage. The Gardens Committee maintains a list of recommended plants for our Pacific Northwest climate.
